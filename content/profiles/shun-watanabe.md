## Shun Watanabe

**Team Director**,

Emergent Molecular Electronics Research Team (Watanabe Lab)

RIKEN Center for Emergent Matter Science (CEMS)

## Biography

Shun Watanabe is Team Director of the Emergent Molecular Electronics Research Team at the RIKEN Center for Emergent Matter Science (CEMS).

He received his Ph.D. in Engineering from Nagoya University in 2011, where he studied condensed matter physics and organic electronics. Following his doctoral studies, he joined the Cavendish Laboratory at the University of Cambridge as a postdoctoral researcher in the group of Prof. Henning Sirringhaus. During his time in Cambridge, he worked on charge transport, molecular doping, and spin transport phenomena in organic semiconductors, contributing to several pioneering studies published in Nature Materials and Nature Physics.

In 2020, he joined The University of Tokyo as an Associate Professor, where he established research programs on electrochemical doping, mixed ionic-electronic conduction, and charge transport in soft molecular materials. His work combined physics, chemistry, and electrochemistry to understand emergent electronic functions in molecular systems.

Since 2024, he has served as Team Director at RIKEN CEMS, leading the Emergent Molecular Electronics Research Team. His current research focuses on the creation of emergent electronic states through molecular self-assembly, electrochemical control, and multi-carrier charge transport. By integrating advanced microscopy, nanofabrication, electrochemistry, and low-temperature physics, his group aims to establish new principles for molecular electronics and soft quantum materials.
